## Incident Details

### 1. Near Miss — Backing Dump Truck (May 6)
- **Location:** DVP yard, King of Prussia
- **Crew/Employee:** #T-03, driver Dave Martinez
- **Description:** Tri-axle #T-03 was backing into the aggregate bay and came within 2 ft of a laborer who was crossing behind the truck. Spotter was not in position.
- **Root Cause:** Spotter (Brian Foster) was refilling water cooler and did not return to position before backing began.
- **Corrective Action:** All yard backing movements now require positive communication ("clear to back" call-and-response) between driver and spotter. Refresher training conducted May 7 for all yard personnel.

### 2. Property Damage — Mirror Strike (May 8)
- **Location:** Greenfield Ave, Lower Merion Township
- **Crew:** Crew Charlie
- **Description:** Sweeper #SW-01 clipped a resident's mailbox while positioning on Greenfield Ave. Mailbox post cracked at base.
- **Damage:** ~$150 replacement cost. DVP will replace at no charge.
- **Root Cause:** Narrow residential street with parked cars. Operator misjudged clearance.
- **Corrective Action:** Added spotter requirement for sweeper movements on residential streets narrower than 28 ft.

### 3. Near Miss — Hot Tack Splash (May 9)
- **Location:** Route 30, East Whiteland Township (prior job)
- **Employee:** Brian Foster (tack truck operator)
- **Description:** Small amount of hot tack emulsion splashed onto Brian's forearm during hose disconnect. He was wearing long sleeves but not heat-resistant gloves at the time. No burn resulted — emulsion had cooled below 140°F.
- **Corrective Action:** Reiterated PPE requirement: heat-resistant gloves mandatory during ALL tack truck connect/disconnect operations, regardless of perceived temperature. Added to daily tailgate safety briefing.
